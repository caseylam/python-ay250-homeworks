The three different alert systems all report slightly different parameters. We follow their naming conventions on their webpages. KMTNet does not provide uncertainties. Names of the form "<parameter>_e" denote uncertainties on <parameter>.

MOA

  • alert_name (MB<YY><NNN>)
  • RA (J2000.0)
  • Dec (J2000.0)
  • tmax, tmax_e (Time of photometric peak, t0)
  • tE, tE_e (Einstein crossing time, days)
  • u0, u0_e (Impact parameter)
  • Ibase, Ibase_e (Baseline magnitude, I-band)
  • assessment (Event classification, e.g. microlensing, cv)
  • alert_url (link)

OGLE

  • alert_name (of the form OB<YY><NNNN>)
  • RA (J2000.0)
  • Dec (J2000.0)
  • Tmax, Tmax_e (Time of photometric peak, t0)
  • tau, tau_e (Einstein crossing time, days)
  • Umin, Umin_e (Impact parameter)
  • Amax, Amax_e (Maximum amplification)
  • Dmag, Dmag_e (Peak to baseline magnitude difference)
  • fbl, fbl_e (Blend parameter (FIXME is 0 or 1 unblended?))
  • Ibl, Ibl_e (Baseline magnitude, I-band)
  • I0, I0_e (Source magnitude, I-band)
  • alert_url (link)

KMTNet

  • alert_name (of the form KB<YY><NNNN>)
  • RA (J2000.0 ?)
  • Dec (J2000.0 ?)
  • t_0 (Time of photometric peak, t0)
  • t_E (Einstein crossing time, days)
  • u_0 (Impact parameter)
  • Isource (Source magnitude, I-band)
  • Ibase (Baseline magnitude, I-band)
  • alert_url (link)
